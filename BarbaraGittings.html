Barbara Gittings

1932 - 2007

Early Life
Born on July 31, 1932, in Vienna, Austria, and grew up in Wilmington, Delaware.
Began realizing she was a lesbian in her teenage yearsdue to her attraction to other girls.
Was rejected from her school's NHS because a teacher suspected that she was lesbian.
Majored in drama at Northwestern University, and rumors of her being gay prompted her to further explore her sexuality.
Achievements
Became a prominent LGBTQ rights activist in the 1950s and 1960s.
Worked to remove homosexuality from the American Psychiatric Association's list of mental disorders.
Co-founded the Daughters of Bilitis, the first lesbian rights organization in the United States.
Organized the first LGBTQ rights protests at the White House and the Independence Hall in Philadelphia in the 1960s.
Challenges
Faced discrimination and harassment due to her sexual orientation throughout her life, but continued to advocate for LGBTQ rights.
Flunked out of school due to her focus on studying the LGBTQ+ movement.
Faced homophobic reactions towards her during her formative years.
Personal Life
Had a long-term relationship with her partner Kay Tobin Lahusen, who was also a prominent LGBTQ rights activist and photographer.
Died on February 18, 2007, at the age of 74, from breast cancer.
Her legacy as a trailblazer for LGBTQ rights and representation continues to inspire activists today.
