Alan Turing

1912 - 1954

Early Life
Born on June 23, 1912, in London, England.
Showed an early aptitude for mathematics and science.
Attended King's College, University of Cambridge, where he earned a degree in mathematics in 1934.
Became involved in codebreaking and cryptography during World War II, and was instrumental in cracking the German Enigma code.
Achievements
Played a crucial role in cracking the German Enigma code during World War II, which greatly aided the Allied war effort.
Developed the concept of a universal machine, which led to the development of the modern computer.
Published a paper in 1950 proposing a test for artificial intelligence, known as the Turing test.
Known as the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.
Challenges
Was gay at a time when homosexuality was illegal in Britain.
Was arrested in 1952 for homosexuality and underwent chemical castration as a punishment.
Suffered from depression and social isolation, in part due to his sexuality and the discrimination he faced.
Personal Life
Committed suicide by cyanide poisoning in 1954 at the age of 41.
His tragic death is widely believed to have been a result of the persecution he faced as a gay man in Britain at the time.
Posthumously received a royal pardon in 2013 and is now widely recognized for his contributions to science and computing, as well as his mistreatment by the British government due to his sexuality
