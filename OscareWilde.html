Oscar Wilde

1854 - 1900

Early Life
Born on October 16, 1854, in Dublin, Ireland.
Showed a talent for writing from a young age and went on to attend Trinity College in Dublin and Oxford University.
Became known for his wit and flamboyant style, and was often the center of attention in social circles.
Achievements
Was a playwright, poet, and author who is considered one of the greatest writers in the English language.
Wrote plays such as "The Importance of Being Earnest" and "An Ideal Husband" that are still performed today.
Also wrote the novel "The Picture of Dorian Gray" and numerous other works of fiction, nonfiction, and poetry.
Was known for his wit and aestheticism, which challenged Victorian-era norms.
Challenges
Was convicted of "gross indecency" in 1895 due to his homosexuality and spent two years in prison.
Suffered a public downfall and was ostracized by society after his conviction.
Became bankrupt after his release from prison and face constant money problems
Personal Life
Was married to Constance Lloyd, with whom he had two children, but was also involved in numerous homosexual relationships.
Was a spokesman for Aestheticism, in the early 1880s he gave a lecture tour in the U.S. and established himself in London circles by his wit and flamboyance.
Died on November 30, 1900, at the age of 46, from meningitis.
Has since been recognized as an LGBTQ icon and a literary genius, with his works and life continuing to inspire and influence people today.
